Part 2: Simulation via numerical integration

  • The cloth seems to take more time to reach the balance state (non-moving state) with low ks compared to high ks. Also, the cloth looks to be more "saggy".
  • When we decrease density, we effectively decrease mass, so the cloth doesn't sag as much. Additionally, since the mass is decreased, the cloth takes more time to reach equilibrium because it has less inertia.
  • If damping is decreased to 0, the cloth will fluctuate for a long time since there is little energy lost. On the contrary, if damping is increased, the cloth looks to be in some kind of thick liquid.

Part 5: Shaders

  • A shader program is a user-defined program designed for shading in 3D scenes by transforming vertices and fragments. After rasterization, we end up with fragments, which these shaders process. These shaders generally take in geometric attributes of the fragment calculated by the vertex shader to compute and output a color on that "pixel".
  • Blinn-Phong, in short, is an approximation of reflected lighting.

  • Bump mapping & Displacement mapping
    • Bump mapping is more of a "fake" approach to texture mapping as it only modifies the representing color but not actual vertices' positions. This might reduce the realistic characteristic of the program be it a VR program, or game, or something as simple as a rendering where objects collide each other. For this reason, increasing mesh's coarseness does little to none to the final product.
    • Displacement mapping, on the other hand, is more realistically immersive as it displaces vertices to fit the actual texture. Thus, increasing mesh's coarseness is worthwhile.
